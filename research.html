Research

I am interested in studying galaxy formation and evolution.  Before I studied the formation of massive stars.

I have used radio telescopes such as ASTE, APEX, and SEST. Interferometers like ALMA and JVLA. Optical observations from  HST.


MASSIVE STAR FORMATION

Accretion in massive and dense star-forming clumps
SMA observations of the hot molecular core IRAS 18566+0408 (Silva et al. 2017, submitted)
Learn radioastronomy (ASTE, SEST, APEX) Learn interferometry (SMA)


DETECTION OF PROTOCLUSTERS

Studied environments of 49 WISE/NVSS dusty, hyper-luminous, moderately radio- loud quasars
Observed with ALMA 870 um
The source counts are >10 times higher than what is expected for unbiased regions Suggest that these dusty quasars are excellent to reveal regions with high density of galaxies (possible protoclusters) Published in ApJ (Silva et al. 2015)


Star formation properties of 0.2 < z < 2.5 merging galaxies at separation 3-15 kpc

Modify and optimize code (Lackner+14) to identify merging galaxies Apply code to HST H160 images CANDELS/3D-HST
Obtain resolved photometry for blended sources in 3D-HST (28%)
Get resolved properties of merging galaxies (z, Mstar, colors, Reff, SFR) Find 110 major mergers
Sample of mergers still in early stage, only 9% are starbursts, low mass companions suffer greater impact on SF.
